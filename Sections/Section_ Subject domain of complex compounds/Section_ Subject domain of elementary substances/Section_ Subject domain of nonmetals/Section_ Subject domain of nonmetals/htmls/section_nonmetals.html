Non-metals are chemical elements with typically non-metallic properties that occupy the right upper corner of the Periodic Table.

Nonmetals include: I group: H
III group: B
IV group: C, Si
V group: N, P, As
VI Group: O, S, Se, Te
VII Group: F, Cl, Br, I, At
VIII Group: He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn

A characteristic feature of nonmetals is the greater (in comparison with metals) number of electrons at the external energy level of their atoms. This determines their greater ability to attach additional electrons, and the manifestation of a higher oxidative activity than that of metals.

Nonmetals have high electron affinities, large electronegativity and high oxidation-reduction potential.
