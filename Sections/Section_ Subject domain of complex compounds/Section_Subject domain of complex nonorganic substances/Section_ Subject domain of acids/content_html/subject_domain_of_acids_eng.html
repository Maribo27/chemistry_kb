Acids - complex substances that consist of hydrogen atoms and an acid residue.

Classification:
  1. By composition: oxygen-free acids and oxyacids.
  2. According to the number of hydrogen atoms that can be replaced by a metal: monobasic acids, dibasic acids, tribasic acids...
  3. By force: strong acids and weak acids.
  4. By stability: resistent acids and nonpersistent acids.
  5. By volatility: volatile acids and nonvolatile acids.
  6. By solubility in water: soluble acids and insoluble acids.
Oxygen-free acids:
  • HCl - hydrochloric acid
  • HBr - hydrobromic acid
  • HI - hydroiodic acid
  • HF - hydrofluoric acid
  • H2S - hydrosulfuric acid
Oxyacids:
  • HNO3 - nitric acid
  • H2SO3 - sulfurous acid
  • H2SO4 - sulfuric acid
  • H2CO3 - carbonic acid
  • H2SiO3 - metasilicic acid
  • H3PO4 - orthophosphoric acid
Receiving:
  • Interaction of an acidic oxide with water (for oxyacids):
    • SO3 + H2O -> H2SO4
    • P2O5 + 3H2O -> 2H3PO4
  • Interaction of hydrogen with non-metal and subsequent dissolution of the product in water (for oxygen-free acids):
    • H2 + Cl2 -> 2HCl
    • H2 + S -> H2S
  • Exchange reactions of salts with acid
    • Ba(NO3)2 + H2SO4 -> BaSO4- + 2HNO3
  • including, displacement of weak, volatile or slightly soluble acids from salts with stronger acids:
    • Na2SiO3 + 2HCl -> H2SiO3- + 2NaCl
    • 2NaCl(sol.) + H2SO4(conc.) -t -> Na2SO4 + 2HCl
Chemical properties
  1. Action on the indicators:
    • litmus - red
    • methylorange - pink
  2. Interaction with bases (neutralization reaction):
    • H2SO4 + 2KOH -> K2SO4 + 2H2O
    • 2HNO3 + Ca(OH)2 -> Ca(NO3)2 + 2H2O
  3. Interaction with basic oxides:
    • CuO + 2HNO3 -t -> Cu(NO3)2 + H2O
  4. Interaction with metals:
    • Zn + 2HCl -> ZnCl2 + H2­
    • 2Al + 6HCl -> 2AlCl3 + 3H2­
    • (metals that stand in a series of stresses up to hydrogen, nonoxidizing acids).
  5. Interaction with salts (exchange reactions) at which gas is released or a precipitate is formed:
    • H2SO4 + BaCl2 -> BaSO4- +2HCl
    • 2HCl + K2CO3 -> 2KCl + H2O + CO2­
